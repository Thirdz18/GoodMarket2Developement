How P2P trading works

Quick reference para sa flow, fields, at common questions. Buksan mo ito anytime para malaman kung ano ang next step at saan napupunta yung G$ mo.

1. Quick start: 4-step trade flow

1
Seller publishes ad — chooses how much G$ to lock, the fiat price, currency, and accepted payment method. The G$ is escrowed on-chain at this point.
2
Buyer places order from Browse — picks an amount within Min–Max, signs a transaction, and gets a payment-pending trade with a deadline (15 min – 6 h).
3
Buyer pays fiat off-platform (e.g. GCash, bank transfer) using the seller's published payment details, uploads a proof, and clicks Mark paid.
4
Seller confirms receipt in their bank/wallet and clicks Release. The escrowed G$ moves to the buyer. Done.

If anything goes wrong, either side can open a Dispute; an arbiter reviews the chat + proofs and decides who gets the G$.

2. The form fields when you sell G$

FieldWhat it means
Total G$ to lock Total amount you're putting up for sale. Locked in escrow until traded or you close the ad. Minimum 20,000 G$ (contract constant MIN_AD_AMOUNT).
Min per trade Smallest order a single buyer can place. Also has the same 20,000 G$ floor on-chain.
Max per trade Largest single order. Must be ≥ Min and ≤ Total. Allows partial fills (e.g. 100,000 Total split across multiple buyers).
Total fiat asking price How much fiat you want for the full Total G$ amount. The number is in whatever currency you pick in the Currency dropdown — it is not auto-converted. E.g. 5000 + PHP = ₱5,000. 5000 + USD = $5,000.
Currency Defines what currency the fiat amount is in. Supported: PHP, USD, EUR, GBP, CAD, AUD, SGD.
Payment method How buyers will pay you (GCash, bank transfer, etc.).
Payment details The actual account info buyers see when they place an order (e.g. GCash 09171234567 — Juan Dela Cruz). Only revealed to buyers who place a trade, not to random browsers.
Description / instructions Free-form notes for buyers — payment window expectations, reference numbers, etc.
⚠️ Double-check the currency before publishing. 5000 PHP and 5000 USD mean very different things — there's no FX auto-conversion. The wrong currency = your ad looks wildly mispriced and won't get takers.

3. Where does the locked G$ actually go?

The G$ never disappears — there's always a path back to someone:

ScenarioWhere the G$ ends up
No buyer / you cancel the ad (closeAd) Back to seller's wallet (full remainingAmount). Requires zero active trades.
Buyer placed an order but never paid → expired That portion goes back into the ad pool (remainingAmount). Seller can closeAd after to withdraw, or wait for the next buyer.
Buyer paid fiat → seller releases (or 48 h auto-release) To buyer. This is the only normal "outflow" of the seller's G$.
Disputed → seller wins Back to seller (status = Refunded).
Disputed → buyer wins To buyer (status = Released).
⏱️ The 48-hour auto-release rule. Once a buyer hits Mark paid, the seller has 48 hours to either release or open a dispute. After that anyone can trigger an automatic release to the buyer. If the fiat never actually arrived in your account, open a dispute before the 48 h window closes — otherwise the contract assumes the trade went through.

4. Trade lifecycle (statuses you'll see)

StatusMeaning
payment_pending Buyer placed the order. Buyer must pay fiat and mark paid before the deadline.
awaiting_release Buyer marked paid. Seller checks fiat, then releases (or disputes). 48 h auto-release timer running.
disputed Either side opened a dispute. Arbiter takes over.
released Done. G$ went to buyer. History is read-only.
cancelled Trade cancelled before payment. G$ went back to ad pool.
expired Deadline passed without payment. G$ went back to ad pool.
refunded Arbiter ruled in seller's favor. G$ returned to seller.

5. Payment proofs

From a trade card, both buyer and seller can upload proof images / PDFs of the fiat payment (e.g. GCash receipt screenshots).

  • Up to 10 attachments per trade.
  • 5 MB max each.
  • Allowed types: png, jpeg, webp, pdf.
  • Stored in a private Supabase bucket. Viewers get a fresh signed URL each time (1 h expiry) so links can't be re-shared with outsiders.
  • Visibility is locked to the buyer, the seller, and (during disputes) the arbiter.

6. Trade chat

Once a trade exists (payment_pending onward), a Chat button appears on the trade card. Use it to coordinate with the counterparty ("send to GCash 0917…", "here's the receipt", etc.).

  • Text up to 500 chars per message + optional image attachment (PNG/JPG/WebP, 5 MB).
  • New messages appear within ~3 seconds (polling, no manual refresh needed).
  • Only the buyer, the seller, and the arbiter can read or post.
  • After released / cancelled / refunded, history stays readable but no new messages can be sent.
ℹ️ Chat messages are stored as plaintext on our servers (auth-gated, but readable by the platform). Don't paste real passwords, OTPs, or private keys. If you need end-to-end privacy, keep that conversation off-platform.

7. Disputes

Either side can open a dispute from a payment-pending or awaiting-release trade. The arbiter (a platform-designated wallet) reviews:

  • Chat history between buyer and seller
  • Uploaded proof images / PDFs
  • The amount, currency, and payment method recorded on the ad

The arbiter then decides: Release (G$ to buyer, status = Released) or Refund (G$ back to seller, status = Refunded). Their decision is on-chain and final.

8. FAQ

Why is 20,000 G$ the minimum? Can it be made smaller?

It is a hard-coded constant in the smart contract (MIN_AD_AMOUNT = 20_000 ether), so even the Min-per-trade has to be ≥ 20,000 G$. Lowering it would require deploying a new version of the contract with a smaller constant — it cannot be changed on the fly.

Is there an upper limit on the ad size?

No. 20,000 G$ is only the floor; the contract does not impose a maximum. You can set Total to 100,000, 1,000,000, or higher — as long as you actually have that many G$ in your wallet to lock, and Max-per-trade stays ≤ Total.

Is "Total fiat asking price" in USD or in my local currency?

It is in whatever currency you pick from the Currency dropdown next to it. If you choose PHP, the number is in pesos; if you choose USD, the number is in dollars. There is no auto-conversion — you decide your own exchange rate.

Can the seller actually lose the locked G$?

It does not disappear by accident — there is always a path back (close the ad, buyer expiry, dispute). The only ways you can lose G$ without receiving fiat are: (a) you click Release before payment actually arrives, or (b) you take no action within 48 hours after the buyer marks-paid, which lets the auto-release fire. If a buyer ghosts you or you never received fiat, open a dispute before the 48-hour window closes.

When does the chat become available?

Once a trade exists (after the buyer signs into payment_pending). There is no chat in the pre-order / browsing phase to prevent spam — use the ad description and payment details for any pre-order info instead.

Can the admins read our chats?

Technically yes — messages are stored as plaintext in Supabase, and the auth gate is enforced only at the API layer. If you need true privacy from the platform itself, that requires client-side end-to-end encryption (separate work, not implemented yet).

What happens to my G$ when nobody buys?

It just sits in the escrow contract (locked to the ad) until you call closeAd. Once you close the ad, the unsold remainder returns to your wallet immediately. There is no time decay, so you can leave an ad open as long as you want, as long as it has no active trades. (Note: yield / staking on the main wallet does not accrue while the G$ is locked in escrow.)

Can my Total be split across multiple buyers?

Yes. With Total = 100,000 G$ and Min/Max = 20,000 / 50,000, Buyer A can take 30,000 and Buyer B can take 20,000 in parallel (partial fills). The ad's remainingAmount is decremented on each successful order.

Why is there a 48-hour auto-release?

So funds do not get stuck if the seller suddenly disappears after the buyer has marked-paid. But this is also the most important window for sellers to watch: if a buyer marks-paid but nothing actually arrived in your account, open a dispute before the 48 hours expire. After that window the contract automatically releases the G$ to the buyer.

Can I upload a PDF in chat?

PDFs are only allowed in payment proofs, not in chat. Chat attachments are image-only (PNG / JPG / WebP). If you need to share a PDF with your counterparty, upload it as a payment proof — it goes through the same auth check.

Who appoints the arbiter?

The contract owner (admin) designates the arbiter wallet(s). On the app side, the Disputes tab is shown only when the logged-in wallet is on the arbiter list.
