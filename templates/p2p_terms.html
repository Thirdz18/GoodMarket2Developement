P2P Trading Terms

Trustless on-chain escrow on Celo. Read carefully before continuing.

How escrow works

  • Sellers deposit G$ into the escrow contract before publishing an offer (ad). G$ is locked on-chain until the contract releases it.
  • Buyers reserve part of an ad's inventory by sending fiat off-chain (GCash / bank transfer / etc.) and then calling markPaid.
  • The seller releases G$ to the buyer with one transaction. If the seller is unresponsive after 48 hours, anyone can trigger an automatic release to the buyer.
  • If the buyer or seller disagrees, either party can open a dispute. The arbiter wallet (managed by the GoodMarket team) decides the outcome.

Your responsibility

  • You sign every transaction yourself. The platform never holds your private keys.
  • You pay your own gas. Gas costs on Celo are ~$0.0005 per transaction.
  • Verify the counterparty's wallet address and payment proof before releasing G$. The platform cannot reverse a release.
  • Once you mark a trade as paid you cannot cancel it. This is enforced by the smart contract to protect sellers.
  • Once you publish an ad with active trades you cannot withdraw funds early until those trades resolve.

Disputes

  • Either side can open a dispute while a trade is awaiting release.
  • The arbiter reviews the off-chain payment proof and bank/wallet evidence you upload.
  • The arbiter can release G$ to the buyer or refund the seller — but cannot send G$ anywhere else.
  • Decisions are recorded on-chain and are final.

Compliance

  • You are responsible for any local taxes, KYC, and AML obligations.
  • Do not use this service to launder funds, evade sanctions, or violate local law.
  • The GoodMarket team can pause the contract in case of a critical bug. Pause does not move funds; it only prevents new ads/orders.
